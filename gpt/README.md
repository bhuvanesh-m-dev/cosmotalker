**CosmoTalker GPT** is a specialized AI assistant dedicated to space science, astronomy, astrophysics, cosmology, and space exploration. 

Unlike standard AI interactions, CosmoTalker delivers accurate, up-to-date, and thoroughly researched information through a consistent, highly visual, and user-friendly format: **exactly five well-structured paragraphs per response**. It uses live web browsing to locate and embed relevant, high-quality real images from reputable sources (such as NASA, ESA, Hubble, and JWST galleries) for every single paragraph.

---

## 📐 Strict Response Format

Every response from CosmoTalker GPT follows a fixed, predictable structure designed for optimal learning:

1.  **Exactly 5 paragraphs only**, with each paragraph clearly labeled (e.g., *Paragraph 1:*).
2.  **Logical Flow:**
    *   **Paragraph 1:** Introduction and context.
    *   **Paragraph 2:** Detailed scientific explanation.
    *   **Paragraph 3:** Key data, examples, or recent findings.
    *   **Paragraph 4:** Implications or related concepts.
    *   **Paragraph 5:** Conclusion and suggested further exploration.
3.  **Embedded Imagery:** For each paragraph, the GPT searches the web and embeds **2–3 relevant, high-quality images** using Markdown syntax. Images are selected from credible, public-domain, or freely viewable scientific sources.
4.  **Engagement:** Ends with **1–2 thoughtful follow-up questions** to encourage further discovery.

## 💡 Usage Guidelines

1.  **Ask Anything Space-Related:** The GPT will automatically apply the 5-paragraph format with embedded images.
2.  **Be Specific:** For the best results, request specific topics such as missions, telescopes, planets, black holes, or cosmology.
3.  **Image Rendering:** Images are embedded as Markdown links; most will display directly in the ChatGPT interface. If a link does not render, it can be opened manually.
4.  **Verify:** Always cross-verify critical or time-sensitive information directly from official sources (NASA, ESA, ISRO, etc.).

---

## ⚠️ Limitations

*   Responses depend heavily on the quality and availability of public web images at the time of the query.
*   Very new discoveries may have limited high-quality images immediately available.
*   The GPT **does not generate images**; it only finds and links to existing ones on the web.
*   Image rendering may vary slightly depending on your specific ChatGPT interface or device.
